What Does it Mean to be a Bystander?

Bystanders of a bullying incident are people who watch the victim get bullied often without intervening in any way. This could be because of the fear that if they stand up for the victim, they may also end up getting bullied. If you are a bystander who is feeling this fear, you can consider asking help from adults or older students who can more easily confront the bully. Keep in mind however that it is better in the long run if you try to conquer this fear so that you yourself can help. On the other hand, some bystanders simply don’t know how they can help. If you’re one of these bystanders, listed below are a few ways in which you can help the victim of a bullying incident to truly make a difference.

What should I do as a Bystander?

  • Try to tell the bully to stop. Sometimes, all it takes for a bully to stop is for someone to acknowledge that what he/she is doing is wrong.
  • Call for help. When seeing someone getting bullied in school, immediately tell a teacher or an upperclassman so they can stop the bullying.
  • Report the bully. When seeing someone getting bullied online, it is best to report the bully to the app or website where the bullying occurs.
  • Comfort the victim. Knowing that someone's there for them can make them less lonely and even empower them to speak up for themselves.
  • Remember that violence is never the answer. Harming the bully in return would only serve to make the situation even worse.
